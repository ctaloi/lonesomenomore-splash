The People Who Matter

Help us understand your loved one's relationship network—who they love, miss, and think about.

For each important person, include: Name, relationship, why they're special, favorite memory together, and how often they're in contact.

Example Good Answer:
"Margaret (wife, married 58 years) - She's been by his side through everything. Their 50th anniversary cruise to Alaska was magical. They see each other daily.

Sarah (daughter) - Visits every Sunday and handles medical appointments. He taught her to ride a bike at the park. They talk weekly."

Other important people, friends, church community? Anyone they talk about frequently?

Pet names, type of animal, stories about them

Passions, Hobbies & Interests

These are conversation goldmines! The more specific you can be, the better.

For the interests you checked, please provide specific details. What exactly do they do/did they do? What are they passionate about? Any special stories or achievements?

Example Good Answer:
"Gardening: Mom specializes in roses - she has over 15 varieties including 'Mr. Lincoln' red roses and 'Peace' roses. She's won ribbons at the local garden club three times. She also grows beefsteak tomatoes and makes sauce from them.

Reading: Loves mystery novels - Agatha Christie, Louise Penny are favorites. In a book club that meets monthly."

Sensory Memory Triggers

Sensory details unlock powerful memories, especially for those with memory challenges.

What era/genre? Favorite artists? Specific meaningful songs? Concerts? Did they play an instrument?

Example:
"Loves 1950s doo-wop and Elvis. 'Can't Help Falling in Love' was her wedding song and she cries every time. She saw Frank Sinatra in concert in 1965. She played piano as a girl."

Comfort foods from childhood? Their signature dishes? Holiday traditions? Foods that trigger memories?

Hometown? Favorite childhood place? Special locations (vacation home, fishing spot, their church)? Places they miss?

Favorite season and why? Most meaningful holiday? Family traditions?
